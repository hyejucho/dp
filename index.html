Concept

Introduction

In college, I see many friends who go to psychotherapy for help. Even if they don’t have much free time, especially freshman year, they rely on someone to help get rid of their stress or set their mind at ease. To shorten their time and help them solve their stress in the comfort of their own home, I decided to make an easy access app through this project. Each person has many different ways to control the state of their mind. Some people drink caffeine, take medicine, or train their minds as well as their body through yoga or exercise. For example, I usually refresh my mind by talking out my problems with friends and family. All these things are more difficult now because of the virus, it’s difficult to meet people in person. I also feel more cautious about sharing my anxiety, because that can affect others negatively.

Being away from home and family has caused my friends to feel a certain anxiety and depression Therapy is very important to them, even sacrificing studio time to get counseling. It seems like it's hard to manage their time receiving therapy while being at school. During the academic year, some people gave up what they wanted to do and returned home.

We know that stress is everywhere around us, and it affects people all around the world no matter their age, gender, or race. Most importantly, one must identify the causes of stress in everyday life and find appropriate solutions and strategies that help minimize it.

Goal

My goal in this project is to make a mental care app that makes receiving the right kind of help simpler for those in need of it. Users can have easier access through the app to their mental care, and live happier lives.

Research

Stress?

Stress is a word to describe the body’s reaction to any change that requires an adjustment or response. The body reacts to these changes with physical, mental, and emotional responses.

De-stress?

De-stress is a word used to do something to become less stressed or anxious in order to relax.



How Common?

Regardless of age, sex, ethnicity and religion, no one is immune to the burdens of stress. Statistics demonstrate the widespread prevalence of this state of mind.





Cause?

Many people need mental health therapies because they are stressed. Stress can be caused by a variety of things in everyday life: such as work, school, parenting, family and romantic relationships. There are also different types of stress that relate to certain life stages: adolescence, pregnancy, aging, and menopause. Children can also be affected by stress and its many symptoms. To deal with stress, or even prevent it, it’s necessary to find out its most prevalent causes.

Prototype

Main Functionality of App

The main function is to help people find ways to relieve their stress. There are many ways that can help to relieve one’s stress, sounds, deep breathing, and exercise, organized by category. Users can easily access it, also can choose the de-stress way according to their taste so they don't get bored.

User Feedback

Through user testing of my prototype app, and 95% of the users could understand how to use it immediately. I got some comments about there being too much text, and not enough illustration, so I decided to add illustrations. Because too much reading can cause more stress.

After the user research of about 80 people, the underlying causes of stress were more common than I expected. Additionally, there were many people who struggled to relieve their stress by themselves.

Design & Development

Logo/Icon



The logo design uses the letter D as the mouth of a smiling face. It implies that when you relieve stress, you will become happier. Psychologically, lavender is associated with healing, relaxation, and purity. The color lavender encourages a calm and tranquil state of mind and is useful for both self-reflection and invokes a relaxed, meditative state. By using soothing colors and simple designs, people can easily remember the logo for a long time.

Typeface



"SF Pro" is a system san-serif typeface, provides a consistent, legible, and friendly typographic voice.

To Maintain User

A mood calendar allows users to write down their mood everyday. Users can time stamp their moods, or enter an average feeling of the day. This feature motivates users to use the app every day, like a diary. Users can also check their friend’s mood so they can motivate and encourage each other.
